Boxing Techniques

Step-by-step breakdown of advanced movements and strategies.

Fundamental

The Perfect Stance

Your stance is the foundation of all boxing technique. Feet shoulder-width apart, dominant foot back at 45 degrees, knees slightly bent, hands up protecting the chin, and elbows tucked to protect the body.

  • Weight balanced on balls of feet for mobility
  • Chin tucked behind lead shoulder
  • Eyes focused on opponent through eyebrows
Movement

Advanced Footwork

Boxing is won with feet first. Master the pendulum step, pivot, and angle cutting to control the ring and create openings while maintaining defensive responsibility.

In-Fighting

Close range hooks and uppercuts

Out-Boxing

Maintain distance with jabs

Defense

The Philly Shell

An advanced defensive technique utilizing shoulder rolls, arm placement, and subtle head movement to deflect punches and load counter shots simultaneously.

Safety Tip:

Always keep your eyes on your opponent. Never drop your hands below your chest level during defense.

Tips & Fitness

Nutrition, conditioning, and mental preparation for peak performance.

Fight Nutrition

  • • High protein for muscle repair (1.6-2g per kg)
  • • Complex carbs 2-3 hours before training
  • • Hydration: 3-4 liters daily minimum
  • • Anti-inflammatory foods: berries, fatty fish

Conditioning

  • • HIIT training: 30s work, 15s rest
  • • Jump rope: 3 rounds of 3 minutes
  • • Heavy bag intervals for power endurance
  • • Road work: 5-10km runs, 3x weekly

Mental Edge

  • • Visualization before sleep and training
  • • Controlled breathing under pressure
  • • Study fight film of opponents
  • • Meditation for focus and anxiety control

Flexibility

  • • Dynamic stretching before workouts
  • • Static stretching post-training
  • • Yoga for hip mobility and balance
  • • Foam rolling for muscle recovery

Recovery

  • • 8 hours of quality sleep minimum
  • • Ice baths for inflammation reduction
  • • Active recovery: light swimming/walking
  • • Massage therapy for muscle knots

Equipment

  • • 16oz gloves for sparring
  • • Hand wraps: 180-inch Mexican style
  • • Mouthguard: custom fitted preferred
  • • Headgear for training protection
